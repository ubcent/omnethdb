## Summary

Define a stable evidence vocabulary for curator-facing advisory APIs so supporting context is visible, transportable, and semantically disciplined.

## Goal

Make advisory review outputs explainable through explicit evidence fields and downgrade reasons without turning evidence into truth claims or implicit lineage.

## Scope

In scope:

- shared evidence fields for advisory review responses
- snippet or excerpt support model
- actor spread, time span, similarity summary, and recurrence summary expectations
- downgrade reason vocabulary for weak, noisy, ambiguous, or contradiction-like support

Out of scope:

- changing retrieval semantics
- automatic synthesis or promotion
- inspector-specific layout implementation details
- hidden write-path behavior triggered by evidence strength

## Acceptance Criteria

1. The contract defines explicit evidence fields that explain why a candidate surfaced.
2. The contract defines downgrade reasons for ambiguous, noisy, weak, or contradiction-like support.
3. The contract states clearly that evidence supports review-worthiness rather than truth, lineage, or promotion authority.
4. The contract is specific enough for inspector, CLI, HTTP, MCP, and gRPC surfaces to reuse one behavior model.
